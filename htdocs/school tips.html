School Tips






School Tips Page contains estudyanTIPS and focuSSISTANT, both of which are a list of tips and pieces of advice that may help a PISAY scholar in her studies. While estudyanTIPS concentrates on assisting the isko/iska with studying strategies, focuSSISTANT will aid you with improving your time management, organization, and focus.

estudyanTIPS

estudyanTIPS is a compilation of studying tips that can help you with creating your own studying strategies. They can be tips about studying, effective reviewing for tests or exams, remembering certain points in a lecture, etc.











  1. Reading aloud can help you understand the text you are reading better.
  2. Taking breaks from time to time is better than continuously doing work without resting. Breaks can help you reset your mood, refresh your mind, and reenergize your body.
  3. Don't always take it easy on vacations given by the school. Yes, we need vacations, but that does not equal always doing recreational activities.
  4. Don't skip meals. Meals are necessary batteries for your body.
  5. Socialize. Having friends and connections at school is a part of a good experience and a support system will help you in your difficult times.
  6. Step out of your comfort zone when it is needed. Sometimes, experiences when you are out of your comfort zone will help you grow the most — it can be a memorable experience, too!
  7. Ask for help. When your own brain and even Google can't help you, try asking others for help. Don't try to solve problems that can be shared with others by yourself.
  8. Highlight crucial keywords if you are taking notes. Remembering these words will help you in your reviews.

focuSSISTANT

focuSSISTANT on the other hand is a compilation of tips that will ASSIST you with improving your time management, organization of notes and files, and focusing in general.











  1. Fix or organize your study setting. A clean studying environment will help you keep a positive mood, as well as an assertive attitude.
  2. Remove your distractions. Turning your phone off while you are studying, as well as closing social media tabs, can help you focus.
  3. Snack on candies, chocolates, or caffeine occasionally for a boost. (Reminder not to overeat! A candy a day is okay!)
  4. Don't cram. Try to do a certain amount of requirements each day — for example, three requirements each day from Monday to Friday while the deadlines are on Saturday.
  5. Maximize your time if you have pending requirements. Don't waste time.
  6. If there is no one to help you study, try researching videos online.
  7. Mindmap what you learned for the day.

Reviewing/Studying
Strategies











  1. Leitner System - a studying method that uses flashcards and time intervals. In this method, flashcards are divided into groups and the student attempts to remember the answer that is on the flashcard. If they are correct, they send the flashcard to the next box. But if they are incorrect, they send the flashcard back to the previous box.
  2. Teacher Technique - a studying method that lets students explain a topic in their own words. In this method, the student gets their own other "student" to whom they would explain the topic to. This way, itwill help the student form their own words and explanation for better understanding.
